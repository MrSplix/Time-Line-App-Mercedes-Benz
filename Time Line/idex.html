Mercedes-Benz Patent Motorwagen

1886

tThe Mercedes-Benz Patent Motorwagen is widely recognized as the world's first gasoline-powered automobile, patented by Karl Benz in 1886. This three-wheeled vehicle featured a single-cylinder engine, a tubular steel frame, and marked a groundbreaking milestone in the history of automotive engineering.

1928-1932

The Mercedes-Benz SSK, produced from 1928 to 1932, is a legendary sports car celebrated for its exceptional performance and iconic design. With a supercharged straight-6 engine and distinctive styling, the SSK remains a symbol of automotive excellence from the pre-war era.

Mercedes-Benz 540K

1934

The Mercedes-Benz 540K, manufactured from 1936 to 1940, stands as one of the most opulent and powerful cars of its time. Renowned for its elegant aesthetics, innovative engineering, and supercharged engine, the 540K is a classic example of pre-war automotive luxury and sophistication.

1886

Renowned for its sleek design, cutting-edge technology, and dominance on the racing circuit, the W25 played a crucial role in establishing Mercedes-Benz as a force to be reckoned with in the world of motor racing.

Mercedes-Benz 300 SLR

1955

The Mercedes-Benz 300 SLR, born in the mid-1950s, is a legendary sports racing car that made a significant impact in motorsport history. Notable for its triumphs in prestigious races like the Mille Miglia, the 300 SLR remains an icon of performance, innovation, and endurance in the automotive world.

Mercedes-Benz 600 Pullman

1964

The Mercedes-Benz 600 Pullman, produced from 1963 to 1981, epitomizes luxury and sophistication as one of the most exclusive limousines of its time. Renowned for its opulent features, including a hydraulic suspension system and a spacious interior, the 600 Pullman remains a symbol of prestige and comfort.

Mercedes-Benz G-Wagen

1979

The Mercedes-Benz G-Wagen, introduced in 1979, is a rugged and iconic off-road vehicle that has withstood the test of time. Originally designed for military use, the G-Wagen's distinctive boxy shape and robust engineering have contributed to its enduring popularity as a luxurious and capable SUV.

Mercedes-Benz 190E 2.5-16 Evolution II

1990

The Mercedes-Benz 190E 2.5-16 Evolution II, unveiled in 1990, is a high-performance sports sedan renowned for its racing pedigree and distinctive aerodynamics. With its aggressive body kit and potent 2.5-liter engine, the Evolution II variant solidified its place as an iconic model in the history of sporty and compact luxury cars.

Mercedes-Benz 500E

1991

The Mercedes-Benz 500E, introduced in the early 1990s, is a high-performance sports sedan produced in collaboration with Porsche. This luxury vehicle combined the elegance of a Mercedes-Benz with the engineering prowess of Porsche, featuring a powerful V8 engine and advanced suspension for a thrilling driving experience.

Mercedes-Benz CLK GTR

1998

The Mercedes-Benz CLK GTR, born in the late 1990s, is an iconic and rare supercar that was originally designed for GT1 racing. With only a handful produced to meet homologation requirements, the CLK GTR became a symbol of extreme performance, featuring a mid-engine layout and a powerful V12 engine that showcased Mercedes-Benz's commitment to excellence in both racing and road cars.

Mercedes-Benz SLR McLaren Stirling Moss

2009

The Mercedes-Benz SLR McLaren Stirling Moss, introduced in 2009, is a limited-production supercar paying homage to the legendary British racing driver. With its striking, open-wheel design, and a powerful V8 engine, this unique and exclusive model exemplifies the fusion of high-performance engineering and classic racing heritage.
